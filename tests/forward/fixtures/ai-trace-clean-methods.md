# Repeatability of a Bench Conductivity Measurement

## Objective

This technical note evaluates the repeatability of a conductivity measurement under one predefined laboratory protocol. It does not claim a new measurement principle or a causal effect.

## Methods

Thirty saline samples were prepared at 20, 40, and 60 mmol/L, with 10 independently prepared samples at each concentration. Each sample was measured four times. A run consisted of rinsing the probe, inserting it to a depth of 20 mm, waiting 60 seconds, and recording one conductivity value. The samples were analyzed using a predefined protocol—temperature, probe depth, and equilibration time were recorded for every run. Additionally, the instrument was calibrated before each concentration block using certified 20 and 60 mmol/L standards. Measurements were collected in randomized order by the same technician at 22.0 plus or minus 0.2 degrees C.

For each sample, the coefficient of variation was calculated as the standard deviation of its four measurements divided by their mean. The reported concentration-level values are arithmetic means of the 10 sample-level coefficients. A prespecified robustness check repeated one sample from each concentration at 21, 22, and 23 degrees C using the same four-run procedure.

## Results

The mean within-sample coefficient of variation was 1.8%. The concentration-level means were 1.6%, 1.9%, and 1.8% for the 20, 40, and 60 mmol/L samples, respectively; the largest sample-level value was 2.3%. In the robustness check, changing temperature from 21 to 23 degrees C changed the three sample-level coefficients by no more than 0.2 percentage points. No run was excluded.

## Conclusion

Under the stated protocol and concentration range, the mean within-sample coefficient of variation was below 2%. This statement concerns the mean rather than every sample-level value. The note does not evaluate between-laboratory reproducibility or performance outside this range.
