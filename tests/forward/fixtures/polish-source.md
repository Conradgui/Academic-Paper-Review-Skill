# Digital Investment and Operating Performance

## Abstract

This study examines the association between digital investment (`DIG`) and return on assets (`ROA`) using 128 firm-year observations. The fixed-effects estimate is 0.37 with p=0.012. The result provides evidence of a positive association, not a causal effect. The manuscript cites the measurement source as [1].

## 1. Method

We use 128 firm-year observations. `DIG` is annual software expenditure divided by total assets. `ROA` is operating income divided by total assets. Equation (1) defines the model:

`ROA_it = alpha_i + beta * DIG_it + gamma_t + epsilon_it`. (1)

The analysis includes firm and year fixed effects. This design reduces some time-invariant confounding, but it does not establish causality.

## 2. Results

The coefficient for `DIG` is 0.37 and p=0.012. In terms of the result, the result is positive and statistically distinguishable from zero in the reported model. It should also be noted that this result is an association. In addition, the evidence does not show that digital investment causes higher `ROA`.

## 3. Discussion

The estimate is consistent with a positive relationship between `DIG` and `ROA`. This sentence is here to say again that the relationship is positive. The evidence remains limited by observational data and the sample definition. These limits are important for interpreting the coefficient.

## 4. Conclusion

Across 128 firm-year observations, `DIG` is positively associated with `ROA` (beta=0.37, p=0.012). The conclusion does not make a causal claim. Measurement details follow [1].
