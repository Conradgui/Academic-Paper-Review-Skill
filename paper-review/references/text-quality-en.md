# English Academic Text Quality

Use this reference only for substantive English manuscript prose.

## Check Dimensions

### Precision And Attribution

- replace vague authorities such as `experts`, `many studies`, or `it is widely believed` with a real citation or a narrower claim
- flag promotional significance, unsupported novelty, and interpretation presented as observation
- keep exact disciplinary vocabulary even when repetition is stylistically noticeable

### Register And Voice

- check unexplained shifts among first person, passive voice, impersonal constructions, and conversational address
- evaluate tense by section and disciplinary convention rather than enforcing one tense globally
- preserve justified hedging and uncertainty; do not remove it merely to sound more confident

### Sentence And Paragraph Flow

- flag overloaded sentences, unclear antecedents, dangling modifiers, and repeated low-information restatement
- check whether transitions express a real logical relation rather than decorate every sentence
- identify repeated sentence openings or structures only when they make the prose mechanical or obscure emphasis

### Candidate Template Patterns

Candidates include vague `-ing` tails, significance inflation, promotional adjectives, synonym cycling, generic conclusions, chatbot framing, and fill-in-the-blank residue. Do not ban individual words, em dashes, Oxford commas, passive voice, or regular sentence lengths. Judge each candidate in context.

## Section Guardrails

- Abstract: preserve information density and evidence boundaries
- Introduction: distinguish established knowledge, unresolved gap, and current contribution
- Methods: prefer reproducible precision over stylistic variety
- Results: keep observations, conditions, and quantitative support together
- Discussion: distinguish evidence, interpretation, alternatives, and limitations
- Conclusion: avoid new data and universal claims

Do not increase lexical unpredictability, inject opinions, add anecdotes, or force fragments to imitate a generic human voice.
